Choosing recipes
A study about a recipe recommender system



Who should I contact if I have questions or comments?
You may contact us with any question or comment at uofg.virtualagents@gmail.com

Should I participate?
You are invited to take part in a scientific research study. It is important for you to understand why the research is being conducted and what participating in the study involves. Please take your time to familiarise yourself with this information.

What is the purpose of the study?
This study is interested in evaluating a recipe recommender system.

Do I have to take part?
Your participation in this study is completely voluntary and you may withdraw at any time without any specific reason as well as without any repercussions.

What will the study involve if I take part?
The study consists of three parts. In the first part, you will be asked to tell us a bit about your food habits. In the second part, you will will be presented with two lists of recipes and will have to choose a few you like. In the second part, you will have to fill in two questionnaires: the first one about what is important to you when you look for a recipe, the second one about demographics. Should you wish to withdraw from the study this is possible at any point without any further repercussions. The study should take no more than 15 minutes.

Will my taking part in this study be kept confidential?
All the data collected in this study will be kept in strict confidentiality. The information provided by you will be used only in research and will not be tied to any identifiable information. The data will be stored in a password-protected file at the University of Glasgow, will be archived and may be uploaded to digital data repositories such as the Open Science Framework in an anonymised form, according to current data sharing practices.

What will happen to the results of this study?
The results of this study will be analysed and interpreted by the principal investigator for a research project at the University of Glasgow and possibly for a scientific paper. All participants will have an opportunity to access a summary of the anonymised data should they wish to do so.
